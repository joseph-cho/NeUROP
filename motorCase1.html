Motor

Case 1:

A 60 year old woman presents with atrial fibrillation and sudden onset of right arm weakness, and her family notices she is unable to speak clearly but she seems to understand what is being said to her. In the emergency room, she is awake and alert, and her cranial nerves are notable. The right side of her face is drooping, with her lower face drooping more than her upper face. She has weakness in her right arm, with her hand having more weakness than her arm. She also has some weakness in the right leg. Her speech is slurred, however she understands and follows simple instructions. Three days later, her neurologic exam looks the same and she is hyperreflexive on the right. She has decreased touch and vibratory sensation on her entire right side. She cannot identify coins when placed in her hand.





Reveal answer

Question 1: The correct answer is bilateral frontal lobe.
